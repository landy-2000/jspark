February, professional surfer Maya Gabeira of Brazil participated in a surfing championship in Portugal. During the event, the 33-year-old athlete found herself surfing especially fast. Although she could not see the wave behind her, she felt it was a special one.

When her ride was over and the wave finally crashed, it made a tremendous sound. Gabeira guessed the wave was the biggest she had ever ridden. She was right.

The wave measured 22.4 meters in height. In September, Guinness World Records confirmed that it was the tallest wave ever surfed by a woman. Furthermore, it was the tallest wave surfed by anyone so far in 2020. It was a significant moment for female athletes. Gabeira hopes her achievement proves how competitive women can be in sports.
